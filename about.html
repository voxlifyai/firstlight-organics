Our Story

It started, like most real things do, with a problem that needed solving.

We had been looking for a moisturizer that actually worked. Not one that felt good for twenty minutes and then disappeared. Not one that required reading a chemistry textbook to understand the label. Something simple. Something real. Something that did what it was supposed to do and nothing more.

We tried a lot of things. Long ingredient lists. Fancy packaging. Products that promised everything. None of them became the kind of thing you reach for every day without thinking about it.

Then we found tallow.

The first time we tried a tallow balm, the reaction was immediate. It absorbed differently. It felt different. And the next morning, skin that had been chronically dry looked genuinely better. Not because of a complicated formula or a proprietary blend of synthetic actives. Because of something people have been using for thousands of years, made well, with good ingredients.

That discovery sent us down a rabbit hole. We learned about fatty acid profiles and why tallow is structurally similar to the oils our skin naturally produces. We learned about the fat-soluble vitamins present in grass-fed tallow and what they do for the skin barrier. We learned about what the commercial skincare industry replaced tallow with, and why those replacements often require twenty additional ingredients just to function.

The more we learned, the more convinced we became: the industry had moved in the wrong direction. Not out of malice. Out of economics. Petroleum byproducts are cheap. Synthetic emollients are shelf-stable. Water is abundant. But abundant and cheap are not the same as good.

We started making our own. Small batches in our kitchen in Boise, Idaho, testing formulas, adjusting ratios, finding the combination that felt right. Grass-fed beef tallow as the base. Organic jojoba oil for balance. Arrowroot powder for a finish that absorbs without greasiness. Organic lavender and frankincense for a scent that is natural and fades quietly on the skin.

Five ingredients. That was it. And nothing was missing.

We shared it with family first. Then friends. Then friends of friends who had tried everything else. The feedback was consistent: this works. The jars started to go faster than we could make them. People were asking where to buy more.

First Light Organics started there, not with a business plan, but with a product that genuinely solved a problem and a conviction that more people deserved access to something this simple.

We are still based in Boise. We still make every jar by hand. We have not outsourced production, changed the formula, or compromised on the ingredients to cut costs. The same five ingredients that were in the first batch are in every jar we make today.

We named this business First Light because we believe that the best things often begin with the simplest gifts. A sunrise. A clean ingredient. A quiet moment at the start of the day. We are grateful for the chance to make something worth trusting and to share it with people who care about what they put on their skin.

Why "First Light"

Our name comes from one of our favorite passages:

"Because of the tender mercy of our God, by which the sunrise shall visit us from on high to give light to those who sit in darkness and in the shadow of death, to guide our feet into the way of peace." Luke 1:78-79

Every sunrise is a reminder that God makes all things new.

We don't see skincare as merely another product on a shelf. It's a daily ritual, a quiet moment to slow down, care for yourself, and appreciate the goodness of God's creation.

Every jar is handcrafted with that same spirit: simple, honest, and made with purpose.

Our Philosophy

These are the beliefs that shape every decision we make, from what goes into our balm to how we run our business.

  • Nature often provides what our skin needs most.
  • Fewer, better ingredients outperform long ingredient lists.
  • Small-batch craftsmanship creates better products.
  • Quality begins with thoughtful sourcing.
  • Caring for your skin can be both simple and meaningful.
